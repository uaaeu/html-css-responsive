Dr. Albert Hofmann

The man who illuminates the millions

Here's a time line of Dr. Albert Hofmann's life:

  • 1906 - Born in Baden, Switzerland.
  • 1929 - Earned his PhD with honors at Zurich University.
  • 1938 - Synthesized first LSD.
  • 1943 - He accidentally ate a trace of the compound and came to know about its powerful effects.
  • 1943 - He deliberately swallowed a dose of LSD and rode his bicycle home as the effects of the drug engulfed him.
  • 1958 - Discovered ‘ethacetin’, which is an 'intoxicating tryptamine.’
  • 1962 - He and his wife Anita Hofmann went to Mexico to search for the plant ‘Ska Maria Pastora’ to identify its active compound.
  • 1963 - He attended the annual convention of the ‘World Academy of Arts and Sciences’ (WAAS) in Stockholm.
  • 1969 - Received the title D.Sc. (honoris causa) from the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology.
  • 1971 - Retired from Sandoz Laboratories.
  • 1971 - The Swedish Pharmaceutical Association (Sveriges Farmacevtförbund) gave him the Scheele Award.
  • 1996 - He delivered a speech at the Worlds of Consciousness Conference in Heidelberg, Germany.
  • 2007 - He shared first place, together with Tim Berners-Lee in a list of the 100 greatest living geniuses published by The Telegraph newspaper.
  • 2008 - He died at the age of 102, due to a heart attack.

"I was completely astonished by the beauty of nature. Our eyes see just a small fraction of the light in the world. It is a trick to make a colored world, which does not exist outside of human beings."

- Albert Hofmann
